A simpler way to manage what naturally collects on the ground.

Leaves fall. Seeds drop. Acorns land. Pine needles accumulate.
That is simply how outdoor environments work.

For decades, we have responded with noise, fuel, plastic bags, and repeated labor.
More effort than necessary. More waste than makes sense.

Netleafa introduces a different approach.

Inspired by agricultural harvesting methods, Netleafa is a passive ground-capture system designed to collect natural debris before it becomes work.

You lay it down.
Time does the work.
When you are ready, you roll it up and move on.

The problem is bigger than leaves.

Leaves are only part of what falls.

Acorns damage equipment and injure ankles.
Pine needles embed themselves into turf and soil.
Seeds, pods, and organic debris collect unevenly and resist raking.

Traditional cleanup methods struggle with this material.

Blowers scatter it. Rakes miss it. Vacuum systems clog or require repeated passes.

The work becomes louder, messier, and more expensive every season.

Netleafa captures everything that falls by addressing the problem earlier.

By placing a thin net directly on the ground, debris is collected as it lands instead of being chased afterward. Shape, size, and density no longer matter.

One system.
One motion.
Less effort overall.

Passive by design. Effective by nature.

Netleafa is a thin, biodegradable netting system made from hemp fibers.

The net is laid directly on lawns, fields, or managed ground and staked flat.

Openings allow sunlight, water, and air to reach the surface below.

As debris falls, it stays on top.

Leaves, acorns, pine needles, seed pods, and organic material are captured together without blowing, raking, or vacuuming.

When the season or interval is complete:

  • Stakes are removed
  • The net is gathered from the edges
  • Debris is removed in one controlled motion

No engines.
No constant labor.
No repeated cleanup cycles.

The system works quietly in the background.

More than debris removal

Debris capture is the entry point, not the ceiling.

The same netting system can be adapted to carry additional functions without changing how users interact with it.

Future applications include:

  • Seed-loaded netting for lawn seeding and repair
  • Fertilizer-integrated netting for controlled nutrient delivery
  • Ground suppression netting for lawn removal or transition
  • Salt-loaded netting for winter ice control on sidewalks, paths, and managed surfaces

Each application follows the same behavior: Lay it down. Let it work. Remove it.

One system. Multiple seasons. No retraining required.
