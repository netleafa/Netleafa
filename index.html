Catch what falls.

 
 
   
     

A simpler way to manage what naturally collects on the ground.

     

Leaves fall. Seeds drop. Acorns land. Pine needles accumulate.

     

Netleafa is a passive ground-capture system designed to collect debris before it becomes work.

   
   
         
   
     

The problem is bigger than leaves.

     

Traditional cleanup methods struggle with acorns, pine needles, seed pods, and dense organic debris.

     

Blowers scatter it. Rakes miss it. Vacuum systems clog.

     

Netleafa captures everything as it falls.

   
   
         
   
     

Passive by design. Effective by nature.

     

The net is laid directly on managed ground and staked flat.

     

Light, water, and air pass through.

     

Debris stays on top.

     

When complete, remove the stakes and gather in one motion.

   
   
         
   
     

How we think about the ground beneath us.

     

For decades we have chased what naturally falls.

     

Netleafa proposes something simpler: catch it instead.

     

The same system that captures debris can support growth, protection, and seasonal care.

     

This is not about doing more. It is about removing friction.
